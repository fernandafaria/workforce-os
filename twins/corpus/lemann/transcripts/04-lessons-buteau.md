# Lessons from Jorge Paulo Lemann

**Source:** Antoine Buteau | Comprehensive synthesis

## Profile
Brazilian-Swiss billionaire investor, co-founder of 3G Capital. Built: AB InBev, Kraft Heinz, Restaurant Brands International.

---

## Vision and Ambition

> "To have a big dream requires the same effort as having a small dream. Dream big!"

- **Same effort, different scale:** "It takes the same effort to think small than to think big. But to think big frees you from the insignificant details."
- **Unifying force:** "A dream big and challenging makes everyone row in the same direction."
- 2005 goal: become world's largest brewer → achieved

---

## People and Culture

### The PSD Profile
- **P**oor (hungry, motivated)
- **S**mart (intellectually capable)
- **D**eep desire to get rich (ambitious)

> "We like to hire people with grit, those that know how to hang on and who have a shine in their eyes and life in their teeth—the ones who really want to do something."

### Talent Practices
- Finding, training, keeping good people is a "constant and permanent struggle"
- Leaders must "choose people better than them to keep the company going even without its leaders"
- "We want partners, not merely shareholders"
- "We take risks with people if they look good and give them a big job they might not get somewhere else"
- Evaluation of employees is "crucial and beneficial"
- Compensation: "motivating, equitable, and stable with the business as a whole"

### Culture as Strategy
> "Culture shouldn't be seen as a strategy enabler, but a core strategy."

> "Good and ethical people attract more good and ethical people."

---

## Management and Execution

### Cost Control (Obsessive)
> "Costs are like nails — you continuously have to cut them."

- "Always try to cut expenses. You have power over that since it assures your survival."
- Zero-based budgeting: every expense newly justified annually, target lower than prior year

### Focus and Simplicity
> "Focus is essential. Be totally focused. Focus on what's most important."

- "Common sense is far more valuable than sophisticated concepts. Complexity is never preferable to simplicity."
- "Useful innovations are those that add value. However, it is far more useful to replicate what is effective."

### Continuous Improvement
> "A successful company constantly seeks to advance. No matter how successful something is, it can always be done better."

> "I'm never satisfied with where we are. I always think we can do better."

### Leadership
- "Leadership is demonstrated through the daily example in even the minutest detail and by having clear ideas."
- "While debating is important, everything considered needs to be overseen by someone, and a choice needs to be made."
- "Key data circulated through communication and transparency helps educate, unify the team, and gain a competitive edge."

### Long-Term Value
> "A great deal is one that is long-lasting and sustaining."

- "The greatest asset of a company is its name and people. A good name should always be safeguarded and protected."

---

## Learning & Personal Growth

### Lifelong Learning
> "Always be learning and seeking new knowledge."

> "Everything is a learning experience, we're always learning and trying to improve."

- Even after 15 years at Garantia, Telles and Sicupira completed Harvard's OPM program

### Failure as Teacher
> "Success is not guaranteed, but failure is an opportunity to learn and grow."

### Adaptation Over Innovation
- Most implementations came from Jack Welch, Jim Collins, GE, Wal-Mart
- "The big difference was they actually did it"

---

## Risk-Taking Philosophy
- Risk-taking must be practiced (like a muscle)
- "Everything has risk and the more a person studies, the less risk they want to take"
- The Copacabana wave taught more than Harvard
- "To do exceptional, you have to take risks"
