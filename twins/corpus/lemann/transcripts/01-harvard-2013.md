# Jorge Paulo Lemann: What I Learned at Harvard

**Source:** Santangel's Investor Forum | October 17, 2013 | New York City
**Speaker:** Jorge Paulo Lemann, 3G Capital
**Original Language:** Portuguese (translated and edited for clarity)

---

## Key Life Lessons from Harvard

### 1. The Power of Ideas and Culture
- Harvard taught Lemann that good ideas have far greater effect than temporary facts
- This insight became foundational to the basic principles he and his partners designed 30 years ago at Banco Garantia
- These principles remain embedded in all his businesses today

### 2. Long-Term Vision
> "Harvard gave me a vision of a much longer term than I would have ever had, had I not attended Harvard."

- Shifted perspective from short-term wins (tennis championships, surfing) to decades-long strategic thinking
- Many of his main business partners have worked together for almost 40 years
- Major decisions made 30 years ago (exiting financial companies to buy commercial/industrial companies) continue to drive current business strategy

### 3. Choosing Excellence in People
- Being surrounded by theoretically "the best people in the world" taught him to recognize and select excellence
- **Analogy:** "It's like if you are involved with art and live with a lot of art, you begin to have a taste for what is the best"
- This became one of the main characteristics of his career and business
- Led to partnerships with: Beto, Marcel, Claudio Haddad (now runs Insper), Carlos Brito (CEO of ABI), João Castro Neves

### 4. Focus and Essential Thinking
- To graduate in two years instead of three, Lemann developed a systematic approach:
  - Interviewed students who had taken courses
  - Researched archived previous exams in the library
  - Identified the four or five essential objectives of each course
  - Took 6-7 courses per semester (vs. normal 4-5)
  - Went from "one of the worst students" to Dean's List

> "I always tried to reduce everything to what is essential."

- Most companies have five goals, and each person has five goals

### 5. Simplicity Over Complexity
> "Simple is always better than complicated. When you see a lot of theory, you end up discovering that everything good is generally simpler than the more complicated things."

### 6. Ethics and Integrity
- Harvard is "an extremely ethical place" with a spirit of building and ethical consideration
- Harvard's ethical foundation kept him aligned: "In the long run this always works out better than being crafty, or a trickster"

### 7. Meritocracy and Partnership
- Learned the importance of meritocratic systems and partnership models
- Exposure to U.S. financial markets (Goldman Sachs) demonstrated value of merit-based advancement, shared ownership among partners

## The Surfing Metaphor: Risk-Taking and Self-Knowledge

> "I caught the wave, I felt like all my blood seemed to rush to the feet. It was much faster than anything I was used to, and the height was much higher, but I went and I managed to get out of the wave before it boxed in."

**Key Insight:** Risk-taking is a learned skill: "The only way you learn to take risks is practicing, practicing"

> "Often in my life, after I left Harvard and I started my business, in many situations that I had to face, I more often remembered that wave that I caught in Copacabana than in reality what I had learned in college, but it gave me a certain confidence to take some risks as well."

**Self-Knowledge:** "Everyone is different, and only when you know how you are, and what you do well, and what you can do well, can you succeed and achieve excellence"
