# The 3G Doctrine: Dream, People, Culture

**Source:** Christiaan Quyn, Medium — Management Lessons from 3G Capital

> "In the end, I am a teacher; that is really how I see myself." — Jorge Paulo Lemann

---

## Core Philosophy: Long-Term Wealth Creation
- Build sustainable businesses with real competitive advantages over the long term
- Partnership sustained for over 50 years
- Nurture best interns into homegrown superstar CEOs
- Focus on sustainability over wealth accumulation

> "If we built our company, then that would be the very best way in the long run to generate wealth. Managing money, by itself, never creates something great and lasting, but building something great can lead to substantial results."

---

## PILAR 1: INVESTING IN PEOPLE

### Pay Serious Attention to Hiring
> "Could you imagine a professional sports coach outsourcing the selection of his first XI team?" — Carlos Brito

Senior management must personally own hiring decisions—not outsource to HR.

### Homegrown Management
- Internal talent factory through internship programs
- Promote in-house talent to management and top positions
- Ensures leaders are cultural ambassadors who perpetuate company values

### Choose Fanatics
- Look for individuals with relentless focus, energy, and passion
- Experience in the specific role is secondary to fanaticism

---

## PILAR 2: DREAMING BIG

> "Dreaming big or dreaming small takes the same amount of energy .. so why not dream big?" — Carlos Brito

### The Continuous Feedback Loop
```
1. Get great people → 2. Give them big things to do → 3. Get more great people → 4. Come up with the next big thing → REPEAT
```

**Critical Success Factor:** People must feel they have at least **80% of the skills needed** to make the dream achievable.

> "If you don't have a new big dream to work towards, you will cease to develop and grow, and you will lose your best people."

---

## PILAR 3: CULTURE

> "Culture is not about supporting strategy, culture is the strategy."

> "We only have one 'trick', which is to put in good people and our management system to change a company's results." — Alexandre Behring

### Meritocracy (Core Value)
> "The three partners believed that the very best people crave meritocracy, and mediocre people fear it."

**Rewards Based On:** Performance (not status), Achievement (not age), Contribution (not position), Talent (not credentials)

### Ownership Culture
- Variable compensation tied to company performance
- Top performers can purchase shares
- "We want partners, not merely shareholders"

### Cost Obsession
- Zero-based budgeting: justify all expenditure from zero annually
- "Costs are like nails — you continuously have to cut them"

### Simplicity
- "Common sense is far more valuable than sophisticated concepts"
- Most companies have 5 goals, each person has 5 goals
